General Thoughts



Redfin was an exercise in developing methods of retaining set class through the transposition and inversion developmental techniques expressed by Joseph N. Straus in his paper Contextual-Inversion Spaces alongside Ciro Visconti’s paper Axis of Contextual Inversion from a cell derived from ideas expressed by Guy Capuzzo’s paper The Complement Union Property in the music of Elliott Carter. The equations are taken from John Rahns book, Basic Atonal Theory.

An idea that seems tangential to the realm of set theory is the literary device of synecdoche, the figure of speech in which a part is made to represent the whole. This idea, I believe, is represented through the CUP or Complementary Union Property expressed by Capuzzo, which in essence is defined as the union of any member of set class X with any non-intersecting member of set class Y is always a member of set class Z. These divisions provide unique splashes of colour, or rather, a modal application of a larger set class.
