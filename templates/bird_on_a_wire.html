for Jazz Big Band



Midi audio representation


Premiere Performances

Program Notes



Initially, I had envisioned a scene in a circus in which a tightrope walker was about to perform. Where the composition would encompass the influx of tension as the performer would play to the audience. Towards the latter part of the development of this piece. The scene had changed to that of a bird traversing a telephone wire. I had seen videos of animals being shocked by touching wires or boxes attached, so that idea was the crux of conflict within the piece. Will they die? Will they live?

Throughout my composition journey, I've always wanted the ability to weave tonal and atonal elements together in creating a grander experience or narrative. I wanted aspects of this piece to feel as though everything was about to unravel, only to be saved by the strong rhythmic foundation of the foundamental idea.

A heavy inspiration for this piece is Jim McNeely's Absolution, Where the quirky dotted quarter note rhythm present in the left hand piano, the immediate introduction of the melody, and the chaotic descending motiffs present throughout that score are utilized here under different context and pitch material.
